# Password Reset — MultiPass Self-Service

## Overview
MultiPass is Mines' identity and access management system. Your MultiPass username and password are used to log in to Banner, Canvas, Workday, email, VPN, and all other campus systems.

## Self-Service Password Reset

### Reset your password online
1. Go to myaccount.mines.edu.
2. Click "Forgot Password" or "Reset Password."
3. Enter your mines.edu email address.
4. You will receive a verification code via your registered recovery email or phone number.
5. Enter the code and set a new password.
6. Password requirements: minimum 12 characters, at least one uppercase, one lowercase, one number, one special character.

### Unlock a locked account
1. Go to myaccount.mines.edu.
2. Click "Unlock Account."
3. Verify your identity via recovery method.
4. Your account unlocks immediately.

### Password expired
- MultiPass passwords expire every 365 days.
- You will receive email warnings at 30, 14, and 7 days before expiry.
- Expired passwords can be reset at myaccount.mines.edu even after expiry.

### Forgot recovery email or phone
- If you cannot verify your identity through self-service, bring your Mines ID to the IT Service Desk in person.
- Location: Coolbaugh Hall, Room 209. Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM–5:00 PM.
- Remote options: call 303-278-4357 with your student/employee ID number for identity verification.

### After resetting password
- Wait 5–10 minutes for the new password to propagate across all Mines systems.
- Update saved passwords in your browser and mobile devices.
- Update password in the OneDrive sync client and any email clients (Outlook desktop, mobile).
- If using GlobalProtect VPN, disconnect and reconnect with the new password.

### Setting up password recovery methods
1. Log in to myaccount.mines.edu.
2. Go to Security Info → Add method.
3. Add at least one recovery method: email (personal, non-mines) or phone number.
4. Keeping two recovery methods ensures you can always reset your own password.

## When to Escalate
- Self-service reset fails after multiple attempts.
- Cannot verify identity via any recovery method.
- Account shows as "disabled" rather than "locked" — requires admin intervention.
